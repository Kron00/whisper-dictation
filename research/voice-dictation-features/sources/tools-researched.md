## Notes on Source Quality

### High Reliability
- Official documentation from vendors
- Apple and Microsoft support pages
- Peer-reviewed research papers
- GitHub repositories with source code

### Medium Reliability
- Tech journalism (TechCrunch, etc.)
- Product review sites
- User testimonials
- Blog posts from practitioners

### Limited Reliability
- Marketing materials (feature claims may be optimistic)
- User forum posts (anecdotal, may be outdated)
- Comparison articles (may have affiliate bias)
